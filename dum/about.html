ABOUT

Who I Am

I started coding in 7th grade. My school introduced HTML and PHP, and something clicked — typing instructions and watching the browser follow them felt like finding a superpower nobody had told me about. I went home and kept going. Python came next. By 8th grade I was building small things just to see if I could.

Most of my school years after that were JEE prep, which is its own kind of marathon. Three years of problem sets and mock tests. You either burn out or figure out what you actually enjoy. I figured out I like problems where the solution isn't obvious — the kind where you have to think sideways to get there. Ended up with AIR 1335 in Mains and AIR 2561 in Advanced, and chose IIIT Hyderabad for CS specifically. BITS Pilani also offered a seat. I picked IIIT for its research focus.

I'm from Gwalior — a city in Madhya Pradesh with a 10th-century fort and some of the best street food anywhere. I miss the poha-jalebi breakfasts there. Hyderabad is louder and faster, and IIIT is an environment where people actually talk about the things I used to only read about online.

Outside academics, I sing, dance freestyle, and play volleyball when I can find a court. I think about efficiency the way some people think about aesthetics — noticing it everywhere, bothered when it's missing. That's what pulled me toward CS more than any single course: the idea that well-written code can make things run better without anyone seeing the work.

A Few Milestones

  • My school introduced HTML and PHP in 7th grade. I built a basic webpage, came home, and kept going well past dinner. That afternoon is the clearest dividing line I have — before and after coding.

  • Switched into full JEE mode in 9th grade. The next four years were early mornings, problem sets, and figuring out how to study something this large without losing your mind. I missed a lot of free time, but I learned how to work on a hard problem without knowing when it'll break open.

  • Cleared SSC from St. Teresa's School, Gwalior with 93.2%. A checkpoint more than a destination — the JEE journey still had two years left. But it confirmed that the work was going somewhere.

  • Four years of preparation, and it paid off. Top 0.1% in JEE Mains, top 1% in Advanced. Also cleared UGEE, qualified for INAO Stage II (conducted by HBCSE-TIFR), and scored 322/390 in BITSAT — qualifying for CS at BITS Pilani.

  • First time living away from home. Gwalior to Hyderabad is a 22-hour train ride and a genuinely different world. The food took some adjustment. The pace took more. But being at a place where everyone around you is genuinely interested in the same things — that part I didn't expect to feel so good.
